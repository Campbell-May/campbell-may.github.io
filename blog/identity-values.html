  • Compassion

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

My family has always been very open to any beliefs and political discussion.
I was raised largely by my grandparents and had a strong sense of family unity. From a young age I was instilled with a strong sense of right or wrong. Not from a particularly religous background but perhaps fairly old school beliefs that my grandparents had.
A strong cultural norm that I think was very challenging but has developed my personal growth a lot is the individualist and rather stoic attitude that I grew up around. Many male figures and friends in my life have had a 'toughen up' kind of attitude. Which never sat very well with me. I have learnt that gentleness and acceptance of ourselves and others is far healthier.
I started to read a lot about buddhism and began to regularly practice yoga and meditation. The values that I hold today I believe have been shaped largely by the teachings of buddhism. Which I came to out of a sense of disconnection with the culture and belief I had been exposed to in my childhood.
