Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability for connections between neurons in our brain to reorganise themselves. Especially from experience, learning or habits. It is all about the idea that our brains are malleable and will react to certain experience. When practicing an instrument for example a series of neurons are firing and connecting. When we practice repeatedly and consistently those neuron pathways begin to strengthen. In general it is the brains ability to adapt. It has a role to play in the formation of habits. Everytime we do a certain action or perform a certain habit we are strengthening those neurons in the brain and further increasing the habit.
Understanding neuroplasticity is very useful to us. It gives us the tools to know how to improve or change a habit.

One of the best ways to increase neuroplasticity is practice. By repeatedly doing something we are reinforcing those neural networks and making that part of the brain more effecient at accessing the information needed to perform the action.

Growth Mindset

Growth Mindset follows from the idea of Neuroplasticity. It is the idea that our personality and what we perceive as our traits are not fixed. That we are constantly changing and have the ability to transform our minds and traits. Our Intelligence is not a fixed level it is something that is much more fluid and we have the ability to study or practice to achieve any level of intelligence or proficiency in something that we want.
I found this exploration fascinating. Just recently I have been listening to a podcast discussing what it is that shapes our perceived personality. How much do external factors like culture, time and experiences create the individual. Or how much is carried in our genetics, is there a level of inherent traits and unique qualities that are present in our DNA that influence the mind. Learning about the increase in ability and achievment of the children that learnt about growth mindset blew me away. It was very encouraging and inspiring to learn about.
One of the main takeaways I have from learning about Growth Mindset is for feeling good about learning. To really take the time to appreciate when I have come to comprehend something new. I can do this by reminding myself and making a note of my progress. Reminding myself that there was a time that a javascript function was incomprehensible to me. Now I am writing them every day. Celebrating these personal growth wins and appreciating that it comes from being challenged and looking for help. Whether thats from Google, my peers or the tutors.
