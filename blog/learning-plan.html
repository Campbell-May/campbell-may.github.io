What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to work in Tech in a way that aligns with my values and passions. Still I am inspired by working for an organisation like Toitu to use Tech to battle carbon emissions, or a company like headspace, making mindfulness accessible through technology.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

The strengths that I first listed in the Foundations Learning Plan were.

  • Passionate about learning. I really enjoy discovering how things work and solving problems.
  • Friendly and Authentic communication.
  • Calm and positive attitude

I definitely feel these are still the most relevant for me going into Bootcamp. I found that I kept a keen interest in learning through foundations by coming up against challenges and having to work out how to solve them. Being really interested in working out a solution definitly helped me stay on track.
Especially going throught the core learnings I was able to bring more awareness to how I communicate. Practicing the Listening and Looping gave me the opportunity to practice active listening and I do feel that I bring an openess and kindness when I speak with others.
Staying calm and positive when faced with the unknown and frustrations was reinforced by learning about growth mindset. I felt more encouraged to be accepting of times where I felt really stuck or blocked. This helped me to stay calm and in some ways welcome the frustration because it encouraged me to problem solve and learn.

limitations

One of the things I learnt in the foundations was that I have a tendancy to feel I need to reach a target of competency. If I can;t quite grasp a concept or how something is working I can spend too much time focusing and getting caught up on this instead of moving on and getting on to the next task. I have learnt that sometimes with the tech things we are learning it is just about repitition. For example working with loops I remember getting really caught up trying to understand how the loop worked in each activity even when I got my code working. It wasn't until after a few times using loops in defferent activities that it really started to click. So I learnt that I just need to trust that it will click the more I expose myself to using different code. Also that sometimes the more I grasp at trying to understand something the harder it becomes to comprehend. Sometimes I just need frsh eyes.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think the biggest non-tech challenge at bootcamp will be speaking and presenting my ideas in front of groups. Just finding my voice and remaining calm so that I can communicate my ideas effectively.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

This draws directly from what I think will be the most challenging. Speaking in front of groups and putting forward my ideas. Also working on my ability to give constructive feedback. I tend to be quite easy going and avoid confrontation. Perhaps sometimes this means I don't always speak up and offer feedback and discussion. Even when others are asking for it and could benefit from it.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My Expectations for the Bootcamp team are the same that I had for the foundations team. To offer support when it is needed and constructive, honest feedback about my work and progress through Bootcamp.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

My expectations for myself in Bootcamp are to continue the way that I have in foundations. To treat Bootcamp as a full time job. Following the weekly schedule on campus and then doing suome code in the evening and weekends to stay on top of the material. To make sure that I work productivley and safely I will hold myself accountable to continue my mindfulness practices and physical exercise. Looking after my body and my mind. I think these things will help me to bring a balanced and calm when I come to Bootcamp and communicate and connect with my peers and the facillitators.
